HOW CAN I BE MORE ENVIRONMENTALLY FRIENDLY?

From 3 R's to 6 R's

  • REFUSE what you don't need.

  • REDUCE what you do need.

  • REUSE anything that you can.

  • RECYCLE what you cannot refuse, reduce or reuse.

  • REHOME what you no longer need or want.

  • ROT the rest.





Waste-Free Lifestyle Tips

Prioritise your purchase: Make sure they can all be reduced, reused, recycled or rotted.
Tell people: Let your friends and family know that this is something you are aiming to do so that they don't give you anything that would end up in the landfill.
Seperate your waste: Keep food & kitchen scraps, garden waste & recyclables seperate.
Recycle everything you can: This includes all unbroken glass, some plastics, paper, cardboard, tin & aluminium cans.
Avoid buying packaged and unrecycled goods: Buy cereals, pastas & other refillables in bulk when at the supermarket.
Say NO to plastic bags: Use canvas & other reusable bags when going shopping.
Take glass or recyclable containers when grocery shopping: Use these for goods that would otherwise be wrapped. Such as meat, fish and vegetables.
Give away what you no longer need: Charity shops will sell your old belongings in order to raise funds for their efforts.
Sell what you don't want to give away: There are heaps of places where you can potentially sell old items.
Shop local: Visiting your local butcher, greengrocer, and farmers market will help stimulate our local economy and keep the dollar on our shores. Plus, you’d reduce your personal footprint in the process.
